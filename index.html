About Me

A little more about who I am and what drives me.

Background

I'm a graduating senior from Potsdam High School while also completing the Clarkson School Early College Program, driven by a deep curiosity about the intersection of human and environmental health. At Clarkson University's Department of Chemistry & Biochemistry, I developed biodegradable bone cement formulations using cyclic poly(methacrylic anhydride) for controlled drug delivery, and I was awarded with the Best Poster & Presentation award at the Clarkson RAPS Conference. I am currently using novel electrochemical sensors to detect heavy metals in soil, with direct implications for environmental and public health.

Interests

Outside of research, I am committed to mentorship and scientific outreach, opening doors for students in my rural community who may not otherwise have access to medical exposure. Additionally, I volunteer to tutor students from low-income backgrounds to make academics more accessible, and my experience as a STEM Education Intern at the North Country Children's Museum reinforced my belief in the importance of early scientific curiosity. Clinical shadowing in family medicine and pulmonology has grounded my research interests in real-world impact, giving me firsthand perspective on how bench science ultimately reaches patients and communities. I am also a USFS Gold Medalist figure skater, a lifelong pursuit that has shaped my approach to long-term goal-setting and the kind of sustained discipline that rigorous research demands.

Diving deeper into cloud-native architecture (AWS / GCP), distributed systems, and responsible AI — especially fairness and explainability in ML models.
