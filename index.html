About Me

A little more about who I am and what drives me.

Background

I'm a rising sophomore Biochemistry student at Clarkson University, driven by a deep curiosity about the intersection of human and environmental health. At Clarkson University's Department of Chemistry & Biochemistry, I developed biodegradable bone cement formulations and am currently using novel electrochemical sensors to detect heavy metals in soil, with direct implications for environmental and public health.

Interests

Outside of research, I am committed to mentorship and scientific outreach, opening doors for students in my rural community who may not otherwise have access to medical exposure.

Currently Learning

Studying how electrochemical sensors function to detect heavy metals in soil, food, and water, addressing the limitations of traditional methods that are expensive and time-consuming. This research sits at the intersection of analytical chemistry and environmental public health.
